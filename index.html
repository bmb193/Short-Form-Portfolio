Course Goals


1. Consider the role of rhetoric, technology, and ethics in acts of making.

In the act of designing, rhetoric, technology, and ethics are some of the most important aspects. I used rhetoric in my designs in order for them to be both persuasive and appealing to users. In my design fuego project, I used a central color scheme to make the design cohesive and appealing to the audience. Ethics were vastly important in the creation of my logos because I utilized the opinions of my audience and focused on how to make the final logo accessible for all users. Lastly, technology played an important role in my designs because I used programs like adobe and canva. Technology gave me freedom to express myself exactly as I wanted to.

2. Leverage Design Principles.


I was able to take advantage of design principles in many aspects of my designs so far. In my cartoon I utilized ideas of contrast to draw the attention of the user. In my good design analysis I used fonts and color choices that lead the user through the design. Learning about the various aspects of design has helped me have confidence in taking risks because I understand the way fonts, colors, and organization affect a design.

3. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

In this class I was given the chance to work with other students to improve my designs and remedy any issues that appeared during the design process. I utilized this time in a group setting to get inspiration from other group members and see how other people utilize their own skills in their unique designs. When working on my design fuego project, two of my team members helped me figure out how to change the sizing of images which improved the appearance of the overall design. Also, when we present our designs my team is always encouraging and often compliments each other's work and we give each other confidence in what we have created. When we created a cartoon of ourselves, it really emphasized the differences in each of our styles, but we could all still appreciate each other's design.



4. Practice revision within and across media forms.

Prior to this class, I was unfamiliar with Adobe programs like InDesign and Creative Cloud. I was able to work with my group to learn my way around these tools and be able to create some designs that I am very proud of. One member of my team is very skilled in Photoshop and InDesign so he was very instrumental to my revision process. He could explain how to make the changes I wanted or smooth out any imperfections in my design during the revision period. I also learned the importance of revision and these tools and my teammates allowed me to make improvements to my designs when I came across issues. In my good design analysis, I created multiple versions with different fonts and organizations which I was able to do because of the accessibility of Adobe InDesign. When given time for revision in class and with opinions from my team, I changed some of the fonts and how they were positioned on the page.

5. Learn to present your work.


I have never had issues with presenting, but it is somewhat intimidating to present a design I have created. I am much more worried about how other people will view the project that I spent so long on. In the first week of this class I was asked to present my design fuego presentation and it was somewhat scary at first. After that initial presentation, I have become more confident presenting in front of a group and it has helped me to have stronger skills in other classes that require public speaking skills. When presenting my designs to a small group I have the opportunity to describe my work on a more personal level and it has made me more comfortable with others viewing my designs.

Course Goals


1. Consider the role of rhetoric, technology, and ethics in acts of making.

Throughout these short form projects I continued to utilize and develop my skills in Adobe InDesign. To create my 2-page spread, I was challenged with replicating a previously designed magazine spread. Through this project, I was required to use new aspects of InDesign for things such as color selection and text formatting. It gave me a deeper dive into the more precise design tools within the InDesign program. I also used Canva more extensively during my client project. I learned how to use the program to create a design that my client outlined. Through that project, I also learned a lot about ethics because this was the first time one of my designs would be applied to users other than myself. I had to create a flier that would be appealing to the intended audience, but would also be inclusive of all types of people. This meant using rhetoric that would be easily understood by all ages and education levels, using fonts that were not confusing, using colors that had enough contrast to grab the user’s attention while also being easy to differentiate.

2. Leverage Design Principles.


I have focused a lot on the principles of accessibility and inclusivity. When I created my movie poster, I intended to design something that would feel serene, but would also emulate the ideas found in the Wizard of Oz. This was a challenge once I chose the background image I wanted to use because I needed to ensure that whatever font and color I used for the title would be easily understood and would not be confusing in any way. I also had to consider ideas about contrast and spacing between and font size which play an important role in the interpretation and accessibility of the design.

3. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

In the creation of my design resume I was unsure how I wanted to organize the needed aspects of the design. When previously creating resumes, I always used a template and I did not really know where to start. During an in-class group discussion a member of my group helped me decide how to create a resume that worked for me. He helped me reorganize the information in a way that created a flow between each section and emphasize the important aspects of the resume. With his help, I was able to majorly improve my resume and create a more creative and better laid out finished product.



4. Practice revision within and across media forms.

In the creation of my movie poster I went through many stages of revision. I created multiple drafts, each with varying font colors, font sizes, word placements, and background images. Going into the design, I had a vision for what I wanted, but no idea how to make that vision come true with the resources available. I changed almost every aspect of the poster multiple times because each time I made a change, I liked the new version better than the last. I went to bed one day believing my poster was completed and in its final form, only to wake up the next morning and decide it needed to be changed again. Prior to this class, I was never able to revise at this level before. When I was confident on my first attempt, I would never give a second thought to trying to make it better. I definitely proved to myself with my movie poster that I have improved upon my ability to make effective revisions in my work.

5. Learn to present your work.


Each time we complete an assignment in this class and are asked to show it to our small group, I become less and less weary of presenting in a group. Being in a small group setting has made me more comfortable with presenting my work and being proud of it. It also has taught me how to ask for help and be unafraid of constructive criticism. My peers can give me insights that have made my work better and they have never torn me down for asking questions or presenting work that was not their style. After presenting these short-form projects, I learned how to explain my design process in a group setting and make other people understand why I made each choice in the design.
